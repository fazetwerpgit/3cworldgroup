# Portal Redesign V3 - Research Flow Notes

This mockup intentionally keeps the current portal feature set. The external sales-team research is used only for flow and layout patterns.

## What Carries Forward

- Existing portal navigation remains grouped as Main, Ops, and Admin.
- Existing content areas remain represented: Dashboard, Onboarding, Sales, Pay Structure, Calls, Chat, Leaderboard, Shorts, University, Links, Recruiting, Approvals, Users, Settings.
- Recruiting and onboarding are represented as website-native flows so recruits do not depend on email follow-through.
- Roles remain admin, operations, entry_rep, l1_manager, and l2_manager. IBO remains a flag.

## Research-Informed Flow Patterns

- Daily work hub: inspired by frontline employee portals and CRM home screens.
- Action-first queues: inspired by sales CRMs where pending work is surfaced before reports.
- Status rails: inspired by sales pipeline and onboarding products, but mapped only to current statuses.
- Split-pane review: inspired by CRM list/detail layouts, used here for sales review and recruiting review.
- Guided enablement path: inspired by sales training platforms, mapped to existing University, Calls, Shorts, and Links.
- Mobile bottom actions: inspired by field team apps, limited to existing actions such as Today, Log Sale, Calls, Chat, and My Path.

## Deliberately Excluded

- No route optimization.
- No GPS or map UI.
- No territory ownership.
- No dialer, call recording, or AI coaching.
- No new SaaS integrations.
- No new permissions beyond what the portal already uses.

## Design Guardrails

- Keep the v2 ops-console foundation: navy shell, green primary action, slate/white work surfaces.
- Use dense, useful screens with crisp borders and practical labels.
- Avoid landing-page composition and decorative AI-style flourishes.
- Add flare through motion, status rails, active indicators, command bars, and polished micro-interactions.
